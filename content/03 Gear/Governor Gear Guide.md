# Governor Gear Guide

**Scope:** Gear equipped **on you, the Governor** — boosts Attack and Defense for **your own** marches: solo attacks, open field, garrison command, and any rally **you** lead.

> [!important] Key difference from Hero Gear
> When you **join** another player's rally, the rally leader's Governor Gear applies to the whole squad — **not yours.** This is the same principle as [[Hero Gear Guide|Hero Gear]] but on the Governor-personal level. These bonuses apply during World Map Expeditions but **do not apply to Conquest (exploration) stages.**

## Materials (separate track from Hero Gear)
Governor Gear uses **Satin Threads**, **Gilded Threads**, and **Artisan's Vision** — completely different materials from Hero Gear's crafting materials + Forgehammers. This means:

> [!tip] Progress both systems in parallel
> Because Hero Gear and Governor Gear use entirely different materials, you're never actually "choosing" between them — you're managing two independent progression tracks. Most players should advance both simultaneously rather than picking one.

## [ONE]OneForAll Upgrade Order
Our alliance's specific slot priority:

| Priority | Slot | Troop Type | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1st** | Middle Slots | **Infantry** | Absorbs damage — upgrade first so your troops actually survive long enough to matter |
| **2nd** | Bottom Slots | **Archers** | Deal the highest damage — maximize second |
| **3rd** | Top Slots | **Cavalry** | Upgrade last |

> [!warning] This differs from the general community advice below
> Most community write-ups recommend keeping all three troop types **balanced** and progressing them evenly through cheap tiers before pushing any one ahead. **Our alliance's approach instead front-loads Infantry survivability, then Archer damage, then Cavalry last.** Follow OUR order for consistency across the alliance — if you want to understand the tradeoffs of the balanced approach, read on.

## Priority Strategy: Balanced Troop-Type Progression (general community approach)
1. Bring **all three troop sets** (Infantry/Cavalry/Archer) through the cheap early tiers (Green → Blue) **before** pushing any single set ahead
2. Keep all three sets in the **same general tier band** — don't let one troop type lag a full tier behind the others
3. Use **set bonuses as your checkpoint**: if any troop type drops below its 3-piece tier threshold, bring it back up before pushing the others further
4. **Time expensive tier pushes (Gold and above) around [[Hall of Governors]]** for bonus event value

This keeps your army balanced and prevents any single troop type from becoming a liability in events or PvP.

## Gilded Thread Rule
> [!warning] Never spend Gilded Threads outside Hall of Governors
> Unless you're one or two materials away from a critical tier breakpoint, save Gilded Threads and spend them during an active [[Hall of Governors]] window — spending during HoG earns bonus event points on top of the normal progress.

- **Satin Threads** are lower priority to "save" — the daily Alliance Store provides a steady free supply, so Satin Thread packs are usually not worth buying with real money.
- **Gilded Thread packs** can be worth buying specifically when timed with an HoG window.

## Governor Gear vs Governor Charms — don't confuse these
- **Governor Gear** → Attack and Defense (this note)
- **Governor Charms** → Lethality and Health (see [[Governor Charms Guide]])

Both are "global" bonuses that apply to every troop under your command during expeditions — but they cover different stats and use different materials, so plan for both.

## Strategic Priority Order
- **Hero Gear** offers more immediate benefit in **Conquest progress**
- **Governor-level gear** expands your **Deployment Capacity** and global war-fighting capability
- For most F2P/mid-spend players, the practical answer is to **work on both simultaneously** since they don't compete for the same materials

## Common Mistakes
- Spending Gilded Threads immediately instead of saving for Hall of Governors
- Letting one troop type's Governor Gear lag far behind the others
- Confusing Governor Gear (Atk/Def) with Governor Charms (Lethality/Health) when planning upgrades
